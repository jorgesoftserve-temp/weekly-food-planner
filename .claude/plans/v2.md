## Context

The Weekly Food Planner today ends its lifecycle at **accept**: a menu is generated
deterministically, a grocery list is recomputed from it, and everything is read-only
afterward. The user wants to extend the product *past* accept — into the messy reality of
actually shopping and cooking the week:

1. **Inventory** with optional/manual expiration and partial-spoilage ("2 of my 5 tomatoes rotted").
2. **Grocery shopping confirmation** — review the whole list or grouped by food group (food group classified for ingredients).
3. **Incomplete-shopping alerts** — when only 30–90% of ingredients were found, surface which recipes of the week are affected.
4. **Cook-status on ongoing menus** — confirm which weekly slots were actually cooked vs skipped.
5. **Leftovers** — a leftovers inventory, manually marked eaten or auto-trashed after a configurable max (default 3 days).
6. **Community recipes** — publish/rank/import recipes cross-workspace so new users don't re-enter basics.
7. **Per-leftover spoilage length** — each leftover spoils on its own clock; every leftover row carries its own editable expiration, defaulted intelligently.
8. **All-members aggregated grocery view** — a single consolidated household list combining everything you'd actually buy.
9. **Menu-level recipe-ingredient substitution** — swap an ingredient *for this accepted menu only*. The grocery list reflects the swap; the deterministic seed does **not** change.
10. **Member switcher on the menu view** — per-member view buttons on the menu screen, mirroring the grocery shop-for picker.
11. **Inclusive (preference) restrictions** — soft **inclusive** preferences (prefers fish, not required) alongside hard **exclusive** restrictions. Both tunable *per generation*, without breaking determinism.
12. **Multi-timeframe recipes** — a recipe can be eligible for *multiple* meal timeframes (a sandwich works as breakfast, snack, *and* dinner), not the single `meal_type` it carries today.

A later round adds features that extend the *same* lifecycle without re-architecting it:

13. **Addons** ([v2.1](./v2.1.md)) — a new recipe *kind* (salsa, guacamole, a dessert) that accompanies a meal but is **not** a constraint-engine candidate. Addons are real recipes (same structure, same cook-mode), optionally attached to a menu (whole-week or a specific slot), and they flow into grocery as a dedicated **"Addons"** section. Plus **on-the-fly cook mode** — cook any recipe (meal or addon) standalone without it being on, or affecting, the current menu.
14. **Extras grocery section** ([v2.2](./v2.2.md)) — manually add grocery lines that aren't from the menu (ran out of milk; a non-food item), in an **"Extras"** section, completing the planner's three steps: plan menus → plan grocery + pantry → plan regular/non-food shopping.

**Community recipes (item 6) is split into its own staged track** — it is too large for one release and
**there is no "collaborative" (shared-mutable) ownership in the system**; community is **copy semantics**
end to end (publish → others import a *duplicate* into their own workspace). The full flow the user
described breaks into three slices:

- **[v2.3 — Share & duplicate](./v2.3.md)** *(the headline community goal)*: a user shares a workspace recipe to the community; others import a **deep-copy duplicate** into their workspace (provenance tracked). If the owner deletes a shared recipe, they choose whether to also remove it from the community — in which case existing imported copies simply **become ordinary non-shared recipes** in the other workspaces (the copies persist; only the community link drops). Includes the **official starter pack** + the versioned seed-pack manifest (same publish/import mechanism). *(Recipe version "see original / revert vs keep current copy" is a flagged sub-feature — candidate for its own slice, needs version snapshots.)*
- **[v2.4 — Reviews & voting](./v2.4.md)**: upvote/downvote, plus a lightweight **review + comment** system (not just a vote), and the ranking signals those produce.
- **[v2.5 — Community page](./v2.5.md)**: the discovery surface — browse + **filter by popularity / most-shared / review ranking** — and a **"My community"** tab (recipes I shared + recipes I imported). *(Tab UX to be designed when we get there.)*

### The one hard rule that shapes the whole design

**None of the post-accept state may feed the constraint engine or change `accepted_seed`** —
the engine stays pure and deterministic (`apps/web/lib/api/menu-accept.ts` proves `accepted_seed`
derives only from slot recipe *tuples* `{day, meal_key, target_member_id, recipe_id}`;
`apps/web/lib/api/menu-grocery.ts` `recomputeGroceryListsForMenu` must stay inventory-agnostic).
Inventory only ever touches grocery on the **read side** as a non-destructive **annotation**
(show required + on-hand + suggested-to-buy), mirroring the existing presentation-only
`applyShopForFilter` in `apps/web/lib/grocery-filter.ts`.

**Deliberate exceptions to "don't touch the engine / seed", all safe:**
- **Item 9 (ingredient substitution)** is *menu state, not inventory*. Because `accepted_seed` hashes
  only slot recipe-tuples (never ingredient lists), a per-slot ingredient override changes the
  **grocery recompute output** but is **structurally invisible to `accepted_seed` and to the engine**.
- **Items 11 & 12 are the two engine-touching items**, and both touch the engine *only at generation time*.
  Generation inputs are *allowed* to change `inputs_hash`. The determinism contract — *same inputs + same
  seed = same output* — is fully preserved, and **post-accept mutation is still forbidden**. Both require
  an intentional golden-snapshot regen.
  - **Item 11** adds a deterministic *soft-bias* to candidate selection and lets the per-generation overlay add/relax constraints.
  - **Item 12** broadens the recipe→slot meal-eligibility check from scalar equality to set membership.

### Decisions locked with the user
- **Scope**: a new **post-MVP "v2" epic**. The 26-Jun MVP boundary is unchanged; these items move out of OVERVIEW "Excluded/Future" into a planned v2 roadmap and are PRD'd now, built after MVP.
- **Recipe seeding**: **community shared + ranked** (publish + upvote + import-copy). Official starter pack = system-owned community recipes flagged `is_official`.
- **Food groups**: **hybrid** — static seed column on catalog ingredients + Claude-API fallback for user-created ingredients, cached on the row (first Anthropic SDK use in the repo, server-side only).
- **Inventory↔grocery**: **read-side, presentation-only annotation** — the grocery line always shows the full required quantity plus an on-hand annotation and a suggested "to buy" amount (e.g. "5 tomato — you have 2 · buy 3"). Never subtracts/hides the requirement, never mutates `grocery_items`.
- **Leftover spoilage is per-item**: `leftover_max_days` is only the *default*; each leftover row's `expiration_date` is independently editable, defaulted from the ingredient's own `ingredients.max_storage_days` when present, else the workspace `leftover_max_days`.
- **Ingredient substitution is menu state**, not a recipe edit and not inventory: a per-slot override table consumed only by grocery recompute; never edits the shared recipe, never feeds the engine, never changes `accepted_seed`.
- **Restrictions are split by mode**: **exclusive** = hard filter (existing), **inclusive** = soft preference (new). Both overridable per generation, including *relaxing* a profile restriction for a single generation. Inclusive prefs are a **deterministic** soft-bias, never a hard filter.
- **Multi-timeframe is a set, not a scalar**: `recipes.meal_type` becomes a *set* stored as a **`recipe_meal_types` junction table** (mirrors `recipe_dietary_tags`). The engine's meal check broadens from `==` to `includes`; slot enumeration is unchanged. Existing recipes backfill to a one-element set, so no-change generation stays byte-identical.
- **Seeding** (refinement): the current 30-item hardcoded seed array stays the *pattern* in v2.0 (just gains `food_group`), then the strategy moves to a **versioned, curated seed-pack manifest** (ingredients **and** starter recipes, offline-sourced & reviewed, committed) in v2.1 alongside the community official pack.
- **AI is a separate v3 POC**: the only AI use *inside v2* is the narrow, server-side food-group classifier (Phase 0). Everything smarter + hosted deployment is tracked in **[v3.md](./v3.md)**.

## Release sequencing — why v2 splits into v2.0 + v2.1

**The cutline is the engine/determinism boundary, not feature-area lines.** Of the 12 items, only
**11 and 12 touch the constraint engine and require a golden-snapshot regen**. Everything else is
post-accept mutable state or pure read-side — structurally invisible to `accepted_seed` and the engine.

Splitting on that boundary means **v2.0 never touches the engine**, so its entire determinism gate
collapses to "engine inputs unchanged + recompute changes only for a Phase-6 override" — no snapshot
regen. The two engine items batch into **one** intentional regen in v2.1 (instead of two interleaved
regens racing against execution-feature churn), and community + the seed-pack manifest ride with them
as the v2.1 growth track.

| Item | Feature | Release | Engine? | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | Food groups (seed + classify) | **[v2.0](./v2.0.md)** | no | prereq for shopping grouping; classify is server-side, never in engine |
| 1 | Inventory | **[v2.0](./v2.0.md)** | no | foundation table |
| 2 | Shopping confirmation + completeness | **[v2.0](./v2.0.md)** | no | 30/90 thresholds, food-group grouping (read-side) |
| 3 | Incomplete alerts (derived) | **[v2.0](./v2.0.md)** | no | no table; reads 2 + 4 |
| 4 | Cook-status | **[v2.0](./v2.0.md)** | no | separate table, seed-invisible by construction |
| 5,7 | Leftovers + per-leftover spoilage | **[v2.0](./v2.0.md)** | no | depends 1,4 |
| 9 | Menu-level ingredient substitution | **[v2.0](./v2.0.md)** | no | menu state; changes recompute, not seed; validation reuses filter at the *route* layer |
| 8,10 | View parity (all-members agg + member switcher) | **[v2.0](./v2.0.md)** | no | pure read-side in `grocery-filter.ts` / `menu-view.tsx` |
| 11 | Inclusive prefs + per-generation relax | **[v2.1](./v2.1.md)** | **YES** | deterministic soft-bias in `assign.ts`; **golden-snapshot regen** |
| 12 | Multi-timeframe recipes | **[v2.1](./v2.1.md)** | **YES** | `RecipeSnapshot.mealTypes` + set-membership check; **batched into the same regen** |
| 13 | Addons + on-the-fly cook mode | **[v2.1](./v2.1.md)** | no | `recipe_kind` reuses the recipe-classification surface beside item 12; excluded from the engine at the *input-builder* boundary, so engine-free despite living in v2.1 |
| 14 | Extras grocery section + non-food | **[v2.2](./v2.2.md)** | no | reuses the `grocery_items.source` column Addons introduces in v2.1 → cheap follow-on |
| 6a | Community: share + import-duplicate (+ official pack + seed-pack) | **[v2.3](./v2.3.md)** | no | publish + deep-copy import + provenance + owner-delete-unlink; cross-workspace **published-read** RLS (additive, never widens `recipes_read`) |
| 6b | Community: reviews + voting | **[v2.4](./v2.4.md)** | no | `recipe_upvotes` (one vote/user) + reviews/comments + ranking signals |
| 6c | Community: discovery page + "My community" | **[v2.5](./v2.5.md)** | no | browse + filter (popularity / most-shared / review rank); personalized tab |

**Flow wins:** (1) one isolated snapshot regen; (2) v2.0 ships the headline execution feature with zero
engine risk and the cheapest verification path; (3) parallelism preserved within v2.0 (execution spine +
substitution + view parity still fan out); (4) each release's test suite is coherent (v2.0 = post-accept
tables + RLS + recompute regression; v2.1 = engine snapshots + addons; the community track owns the
cross-workspace published-read RLS, staged across v2.3–v2.5).

**Seed-pack seam:** v2.0's Phase 0 keeps extending the **existing inline 30-item array** (adds the
`food_group` column + values); the **versioned manifest** (`ingredients.json` + `recipes.json`) defers to
**v2.3** with the community official starter pack (same publish/import mechanism). No orphaned dependency.

**Grocery-source seam (v2.1 → v2.2):** Addons (v2.1) introduces `grocery_items.source {meal,addon,extra}`
to give menu-attached addon ingredients their own "Addons" section in the grocery list. **Extras (v2.2)
is then nearly free** — it just adds the `extra` source value plus a manual "add to this week's list" UI
(catalog-linked or free-text for non-food). Deferring Extras costs almost nothing because the hard part —
sectioned grocery lines that don't originate from a meal slot — already shipped in v2.1.

## v3 — split out

The AI-assist POC (auto-grouping, i18n, voice/NL capture, metadata prefill, further UX ideas) and the
hosted-deployment work live in their own roadmap: **[v3.md](./v3.md)**. Research / not-committed, gated
until after v2.0 ships (its food-group classifier proves the "Claude-on-server" pattern). Nothing in v3
touches the engine's determinism — AI suggests *inputs* a human confirms; the engine still runs pure.
